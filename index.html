What is Art ?

Isn't art simple Straight lines, circles and semi circles interacting with one another to create an illusion of depth and perception of objects?

  • Get Started

Study

Study an object - shapes and sizes of objects, proximity to each other and their interactions, before representing it on paper or another medium. Understanding these lets one understand reality. Dismantling the object into its component lines in your mind to see how it is made. Perception matters. Depth is created in the way it is perceived.

Perspectives

There are many types of perspectives. Single, to multiple vanishing points. Perspectives create an illusion of depth. Perspective in an artwork must include coherent interaction of objects on the canvas. Realistic representation of depth is only possible when objects are depicted naturally within a set perspective.

Values

Variation in shades allow viewers to have an immersive experience. With proper shading and choice of perspective, an illusion of three dimensions can be recreated on a canvas. Clear mid-tones, shadows and light areas shows a level of maturity in the art.

Lastly...

Tricks like using projectors and tracing papers may get the job done, but a tendency to use such tricks may not improve one’s skill. It may be commercially viable to many as a short term solution, but employing such techniques can seriously limit one’s ability to do more.
